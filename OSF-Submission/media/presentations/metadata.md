## Title

Nature Foundation Models, Decision Biology, and A-S-R-A — Adaptive Scientific Intelligence

## Description

A technical overview (~10 min) of the research stack behind adaptive scientific intelligence: why current AI is insufficient for scientific discovery, what Nature Foundation Models aim to build, how Decision Biology reframes cells as information-processing agents, and how A-S-R-A (Adaptive Scientific Reasoning Architecture) learns hidden action semantics and evolving world models through interaction—without explicit semantic supervision.

The video covers the full architecture: multi-scale NFM reasoning, signaling and perturbation dynamics in biology, ASRA’s seven-component engine (observation, memory, semantics inference, world model, hypothesis generation, experimentation, abstraction), and the observe → hypothesize → intervene → evaluate → abstract loop. It closes on the shift from descriptive science to autonomous, intervention-driven scientific intelligence.
