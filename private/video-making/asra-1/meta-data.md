## Title

A-S-R-A, Decision Biology, and Nature Foundation Models — Adaptive Scientific Intelligence

## Description

A concise overview of the A-S-R-A research stack: why static AI is not enough for scientific discovery, how the Adaptive Scientific Reasoning Architecture works as an exploration-driven reasoning engine, and how Decision Biology and Nature Foundation Models extend that foundation across science.

The video walks through the core hierarchy—A-S-R-A as the adaptive reasoning substrate, Decision Biology as the first scientific domain, and Nature Foundation Models as the broader ecosystem for multi-domain intelligence—and explains hidden-mechanics inference, experimentation, cellular information processing, and the path toward scientific agents and discovery infrastructure.

Topics covered:
- Limits of task-specific and benchmark-bound AI
- Science as an observe → experiment → infer → adapt loop
- A-S-R-A: reasoning in unfamiliar environments
- Decision Biology: cells as decision-makers under uncertainty
- Nature Foundation Models: beyond biology into unified scientific intelligence
- Future phases: semantics, world models, strategy, and scientific reasoning

Before intelligence can reason, it must learn to observe, experiment, and remember.
